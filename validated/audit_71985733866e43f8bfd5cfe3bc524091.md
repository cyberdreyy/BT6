### Title
`revokePusher()` revocation is bypassable via signature replay in `allowPushers()`, enabling silent feed hijack — (`smart-contracts-poc/contracts/oracles/compressed/CompressedOracle.sol`)

---

### Summary

`revokePusher()` sets `namespaceRemapping[pusher] = address(0)`, but `allowPushers()` contains no nonce, no used-signature tracking, and no check that the pusher has already revoked. A creator who holds a still-valid (pre-deadline) EIP-191 signature can immediately replay it after the pusher revokes, silently re-establishing the delegation. The pusher's revocation is rendered permanently ineffective until the deadline expires, and the pusher's subsequent price pushes — intended for their own namespace — are silently redirected into the creator's namespace and consumed by any pool bound to that creator's feed.

---

### Finding Description

`allowPushers()` requires the pusher's EIP-191 signature over `(chainid, oracle, deadline, pusher, creator)` and calls `_ensureDeadline(deadline)` to reject expired signatures: [1](#0-0) 

The signed message binds chain, oracle, deadline, pusher, and creator — but contains **no nonce and no revocation flag**. Once the pusher signs, the signature is valid for any number of `allowPushers()` calls until `block.timestamp > deadline`.

`revokePusher()` zeroes the mapping: [2](#0-1) 

Because `allowPushers()` only checks `pusher == ECDSA.recover(hash, sig)` and `block.timestamp <= deadline`, the creator can immediately call `allowPushers()` again with the **identical signature** to restore `namespaceRemapping[pusher] = creator`. The pusher can call `revokePusher()` again; the creator replays again. This is an unbounded loop where the creator always wins until the deadline passes.

The `fallback()` push path resolves the effective namespace as: [3](#0-2) 

When the delegation is silently re-established, the pusher — believing they have revoked — may begin pushing prices intended for their own namespace. Those pushes are written into the creator's namespace instead, at the same `slotId`. Any pool whose `feedId` encodes the creator's address and that `slotId` will consume the pusher's prices for the wrong asset pair.

This is structurally identical to H-04: the guard (`namespaceRemapping[pusher] == address(0)`) is reset by a legitimate lifecycle action (`revokePusher`), and the privileged actor (creator) exploits the zeroed state to bypass the guard and redirect a resource (price feed) they are no longer authorized to control.

Note: `allowContractPushers()` is **not** affected — it re-executes a live `isPusher()` staticcall on every invocation, so a contract pusher that returns `false` cannot be re-delegated without its consent. [4](#0-3) 

---

### Impact Explanation

A pool whose `PriceProvider` or `AnchoredPriceProvider` reads from the creator's `feedId` will receive prices pushed by the hijacked pusher for a **different asset**. The `fallback()` monotonicity check only rejects older timestamps; it does not validate that the price belongs to the expected asset. A swap executed against a wrong-asset price causes the pool to settle at an incorrect rate, resulting in direct loss of trader principal or LP assets. In the minimum-harm case the pusher stops pushing after revocation, the feed goes stale, and the staleness guard halts the pool — a broken core functionality impact.

---

### Likelihood Explanation

The trigger requires a creator who is malicious or compromised and who retains the pusher's original signature (a normal operational artifact). The pusher must have signed a delegation with a non-trivial deadline (common for operational convenience). No on-chain setup beyond the original `allowPushers()` call is needed. The pusher has no on-chain mechanism to invalidate the signature before the deadline; their only recourse is off-chain key rotation, which does not affect the already-signed message.

---

### Recommendation

Add a per-pusher revocation nonce stored in a mapping (e.g., `mapping(address => uint256) public pusherNonce`) that is incremented by `revokePusher()` and `removePushers()`. Include the nonce in the signed digest:

```solidity
keccak256(abi.encode(block.chainid, address(this), deadline, pusher, msg.sender, pusherNonce[pusher]))
```

After revocation increments `pusherNonce[pusher]`, all previously issued signatures become cryptographically invalid. Alternatively, maintain a `mapping(bytes32 => bool) usedSignatures` and mark each signature hash as consumed on first use, rejecting any replay.

---

### Proof of Concept

1. Pusher P signs: `sig = sign(keccak256(abi.encode(chainid, oracle, deadline=T+365days, P, C)))` and hands it to creator C.
2. C calls `allowPushers(T+365days, [P], [sig])` → `namespaceRemapping[P] = C`. P's pushes go to C's namespace.
3. P decides to stop serving C and calls `revokePusher()` → `namespaceRemapping[P] = address(0)`. P believes they are now pushing to their own namespace.
4. C immediately calls `allowPushers(T+365days, [P], [sig])` with the **same signature** → `namespaceRemapping[P] = C` again. No new consent from P is required.
5. P, unaware of step 4, begins pushing ETH/USD prices at `slotId=0` for their own namespace.
6. Those pushes are written to C's namespace at `slotId=0`. C's pool reads `feedIdOf(C, 0, 0)` expecting BTC/USD.
7. The pool executes swaps at ETH/USD prices for a BTC/USD pool → traders receive incorrect amounts → direct loss of principal.
8. P calls `revokePusher()` again; C replays `sig` again. The cycle repeats until `block.timestamp > T+365days`. [5](#0-4) [2](#0-1) [6](#0-5)

        _ensureDeadline(deadline);

        uint256 l = pushers.length;
